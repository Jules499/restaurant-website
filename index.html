We provide the authentic taste of Aotearoa New Zealand.

Kai (food) is considered to come from the atua (gods) and there are many spiritual components to the processes of gathering and preparing kai. Kai is used to lift tapu (sacredness) in many rituals and manaakitanga (hospitality) is demonstrated at hui (gatherings) by providing lavish kai.

Cultural Experience

Experience Māori culture.

Karakia

A karakia is a traditional Māori prayer that is often said before a meal. It is a way to show respect for the food we are about to eat and to give thanks for the people and resources that made it possible. Saying a karakia before a meal is an important part of Māori culture, and it is a way to honor our ancestors and the land. It is also a way to connect with each other and to celebrate the food that nourishes us.
